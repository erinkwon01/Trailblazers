Climate

Summer starts in December, similar to Buenos Aires, however the weather is between 60-70 degrees Farenheit year-round.


Culture

Physical beauty is very important in Brazil; preference for casual clothing/beachwear that shows off the wearer’s body in the best way
People in Rio are very extroverted, very loud and animated; interruptions and interjections aren’t necessarily considered rude
If you are invited to someone’s home, bring a small gift, maybe even send one the day after to express gratitude.
Music and dance are a big part of everyday life in Brazil; Carnaval is a very famous festival that they have once a year in Rio.
When meeting strangers or friends, men will exchange firm handshakes and a clap on the back, whereas women will greet each other with air kisses on both cheeks.
Be wary of actually touching the woman's cheek - in Rio it is easily misinterpreted as more than platonic.






Landmarks




  • Mirante Dona Marta

  • Cliffside lookout point offering expansive, panoramic views of Rio & the Christ the Redeemer statue.
    •









  • Cristo Redentor

  • An art deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio, created by French sculptor Paul Landowski;
  • it was built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot.
  • Constructed between 1922 and 1931, the statue is 30 metres (98 ft) high, excluding its 26 foot pedestal. The arms stretch 28 metres (92 ft) wide.







  • Fort Copacabana

  • A military base at the south end of the beach that defines the district of Copacabana, Rio.
  • The base is open to the public and contains the Army Historical Museum and a coastal defense fort that is the actual Fort Copacabana.
    •










  • Vista Chinesa

  • Vista Chinesa is a monument located in Tijuca National Park in Rio de Janeiro.
  • The monument itself is a bamboo gazebo on the ascent of Alto da Boa Vista.
