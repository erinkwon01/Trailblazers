Lima


About Lima

(12.0464° S, 77.0428° W)
Time zone: Peru standard time zone (GMT-5:00)
Currency: "Peruvian Sol" (1 sol = 0.30 USD) Lima is the capital and the largest city of Peru.
It is located in the valleys of the Chillón, Rímac and Lurín rivers, in the central coastal part of the country, overlooking the Pacific Ocean.


Climate

Despite it's tropical location, Lima's proximity to the Pacific leads to a cooler climate. Summer is from December to April and Winter is from June to October.
The daily temperature is generally between 60s-80s degrees Farenheit.



Culture

It's best to avoid revealing clothes, especially in places like churches.
Discussion of local politics, past presidents/corruption/terrorism is fine but avoid topics like drugs and religion because they can be very sensitive
It is of the upmost importance that you respect the native people in Peru, as the majority of the population is Amerindian.
Do not refer to them as "indians", since that term is seen as derogatory - instead, use "indigenous".






Landmarks




  • El Circuito Magico del Agua

  • Proceeds from this entrance fee were once being used to renovate the Municipal Theatre of Lima, which has since been reopened.
    •
  • It is currently the world record holder for the largest fountain complex in the world, consisting of 13 distinct fountains. All of the fountains are lit at night, many with continuously changing color schemes.









  • Miraflores

  • It is one of the most affluent districts in Lima. It has various hotels restaurants, bars, nightclubs, and department stores. Miraflores is one of the main tourist attractions in Lima.







  • Hacienda Mamacona

  • This is a Peruvian horse and music show in Lima, performed by a group of horsemen with over 35 years of experience doing this.










  • Casa de Aliaga

  • It was constructed on the date, that is shared with that of the foundation of the city, it has been inhabited permanently by the descendants of the first proprietor, Jerónimo de Aliaga, standard-bearer and exchequer for Francisco Pizarro.
  • This home is the oldest mansion of the city and faces the Government Palace, the former seat of Pizarro's rule.
