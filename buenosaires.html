Buenos Aires, Argentina


About Buenos Aires

(34.6037° S, 58.3816° W)
Time zone: Argentina standard time zone (GMT-3:00)
Currency: Argentine peso (1 Argentine peso = 0.023 USD) Buenos Aires is the capital city of Argentina.
The city is coextensive with the Federal District and is situated on the shore of the Río de la Plata. Buenos Aires is Latin America’s national centre of commerce, industry, politics, culture, and technology.
It is also one of Latin America's most important ports and populous cities.


Climate

Summer starts in December; winter starts in June. The summer vacation months in Argentina are January and February.
The weather is pretty humid in summer (50°F - 80°F),
there may be some rainfall around March towards the end of the summer.
The most beautiful time to visit is October and November when the jacaranda trees are in bloom.



Culture

Spanish is the main language spoken in Buenos Aires,
however it is different than the Spanish spoken in Spain.
French, German, English, and Italian can also be heard around Argentina.
Mate ("mah-tay") is a bitter tea drink that is shared among friends; it is a very social aspect of everyday life.
When meeting people (strangers or friends) one kiss on the right cheek is customary.
Argentinians are very loud and animated - think Italians. They aren't on time to anything, and don't like strict plans.
As a society, they are polarized when it comes to politics, soccer, and religion - simple conversations can become sensitive topics very easily.
Siestas are every day from 12:30 until 4-5pm; almost everything will be closed during this time. Expect late dinners to follow the siestas.






Landmarks




  • Catedral Metropolitana

  • The main Catholic church in Buenos Aires, Argentina.
  • It is located in the city center, overlooking Plaza de Mayo, on the corner of San Martín and Rivadavia streets, in the San Nicolás neighbourhood.
  • It is the mother church of the Archdiocese of Buenos Aires and the primatial church of Argentina.










  • Casa Rosada

  • The executive mansion and office of the President of Argentina.
  • The palatial mansion is known officially as "Casa".







  • Fort Copacabana

  • A landmark office building in the neighborhood of Monserrat, Buenos Aires.
  • When it was built it was the tallest building in the city and South America.
  • Its twin brother, Palacio Salvo, is a building of greater height, built by the same architect in Montevideo.










  • Congreso de la Nacion Argentina

  • The seat of the Argentine National Congress, located in Buenos Aires at the western end of Avenida de Mayo.
  • Constructed between 1898 and 1906, the palace is a National Historic Landmark.
