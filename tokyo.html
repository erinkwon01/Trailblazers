Tokyo, Japan


About Tokyo

(35.6762° N, 139.6503° E)
Time zone: Japan Standard Time JST (JST + 9:00)
Currency: Japan ‘yen’ (1 yen = 0.0092 USD)
Tokyo is a major commercial and administrative centre with sky high buildings.
It is a famous place for tourists to visit.


Climate

Japan has four very distinct seasons:
The summer is very hot and humid (75°F - 90°F).
There is around three to four weeks of rain, however it's still very humid and hot outside but the rain
is very productive for the farmers growing rice fields.

In the winter it is pretty mild with the weather but there are very cold days (34°F - 50°F).

The spring is one of the prettiest times of year where pink cherry blossoms grow, however they only last about 2-3 weeks.
The weather is very nice, but can get a little chilly at times (39°F - 73°F).

Fall is also a very beautiful season where the leaves on the trees turn red, yellow and orange.
The weather can be hot and humid at first but it does get colder as the months go by (46°F - 81°F).


Culture

The native people in Tokyo have accustomed to foreigners from all over the world,
however the elderlies and children in Tokyo are not proud of having tourists.
Native people living in Tokyo are very polite and courteous.
When entering a home in any part of Japan you must take your shoes off before stepping inside the house.
There is a rule people in Tokyo don’t have the opportunity to speak english,
unless they are employees in hotels or large shopping malls.
Japanese favorite cuisines is seafood, but traditional Japanese food is called Washoku.
Some older tradition that should be known is to never step on a tatami (a small mat made of reeds)
with shoes on, it is considered as very bad behavior.
Before stepping into public swimming pools or onsen (hot spring) always take a shower first
(showers are always located near the pool or onsen).






Landmarks




  • Tokyo Skytree

  • It is the tallest tower and the second tallest structure in the world.
  • The purpose of the tower is broadcasting, restaurants, and observation, and it is located in Sumida Tokyo.
  • The designer of the tower made it with three concepts: traditional beauty of Japan, new life and vitality of the city, and safety and security.
  • The tower was made with very strong steel tubes surrounding a concrete column in the middle.
  • Although admission is not free and is an extra charge to go to the very top,
  • the views are very beautiful, especially at sunset with nice skyhigh city views from the top of the structure.
  • There are 3 small cafes called Tembo Deck Floor 340 ($$), Tembo Deck Floor Cafe 350 ($$), Tembo Deck Floor 345($$).









  • Tokyo Tower

  • It is the second largest structure in Japan.
  • The designer of the Tokyo Tower got his inspiration
  • from the Eiffel Tower in Paris, France.
  • It is made from prefabricated steel, and although the structure is taller than the Eiffel Tower the advanced steel technology made the structure half the weight of the Eiffel Tower.
  • The tower controls the broadcasting signals for television, radio, reception, transmission, traffic information, weather and air pollution data in the metropolitan area.
  • A wax museum of pop culture icons is held in the Tokyo Tower,
  • which was shipped all the way from London.
  • The Tokyo Tower has free admission to go and observe the city from the 45th floor, which is 202 meter above the ground.







  • Ginza

  • It is a part of Tokyo but it has all the
  • features of the luxury side of hollywood.
  • A famous parlor called Shibuya Nishimura Fruits Parlor Dogenzaka ($$),
  • they have very good good fruit parfaits that melt in your mouth.
  • If you go too late there can be a long line but it is worth the wait!










  • Senso-ji

  • It is a buddhist temple located in Asakusa and is one of the most colorful and popular temple.
  • The legend says that in the year 628,
  • two brothers fished a statue of Kannon, the goddess of mercy,
  • out of the Sumida River, and even though they
  • put the statue back into the river,
  • it always returned to them. It was built
  • nearby for the goddess of Kannon.
  • The temple was completed in 645, making it Tokyo's oldest temple.
