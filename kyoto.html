Kyoto, Japan


About Kyoto

(35.0116° N, 135.7681° E)
Time zone: Japan Standard Time JST (JST + 9:00)
Currency: Japan ‘yen’ (1 yen = 0.0092 USD)
In 794 - 1868 Kyoto was the capital of japan, the city lies on the island of Honshu.
It is most famous for its classic traditional Bhuddist temples, as well as gardens, Imperial palaces,
Shinto Shrines and traditional wooden houses.


Climate

In the summer there are about 2 weeks of rain but the weather is very hot and humid even on rainy days (66° - 92°)
many tourists go because of summer break but the weather can be hard to get used to.

The fall is one of the prettiest seasons with yellow, orange, and red leaves growing on the trees.
Many tourists go around the months of October or November because of the great weather mild weather (46° - 84°).

Spring is another beautiful season in Japan, when all the pink cherry blossoms grow,
however they only last about 2-3 weeks (39° - 77°).
The weather is pretty mid temperature but some days can be quite chilly.

The spring is one of the prettiest times of year where pink cherry blossoms grow, however they only last about 2-3 weeks.
The weather is very nice, but can get a little chilly at times (39°F - 73°F).

In the winter it is pretty mild with the weather but there are very cold days (34°F - 50°F).


Culture

Since Kyoto is the original capital a lot of the original traditions are still highly respected.
A Japaesne traditional attire called Kimono is still worn in some parts of Kyoto,
they are very colorful layered robes with a cloth belt tied on the back.
Tea ceremonies of making green tea is very traditional and many parts of Japan still has this traditions.
Shodo calligraphy is an ancient way of writing letters with ink and a brush.
Japanese favorite cuisines is seafood, but traditional Japanese food is called Washoku.
Some older tradition that should be known is to never step on a tatami (a small mat made of reeds)
with shoes on, it is considered as very bad behavior.
Before stepping into public swimming pools or onsen (hot spring) always take a shower first.
(showers are always located near the pool or onsen).






Landmarks




  • Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

  • This golden pavilion is a zen temple in northern temple.
  • This temples top 2 stories are covered completely in real gold leaf,
  • and the temple is overlooking a large pond.
  • This temple has been burned down several times in the past, although this current version was built in 1955.
  • The 2 first two stories were built in different styles,
  • the first floor was built in Shinden style (wood pillars with white plaster walls)
  • and the second floor is made built in Bukke style (exterior is covered in gold leaf).









  • Arashiyama

  • The fist part of Arashiyama is the main area of tourist crowds,
  • however if you go upwards toward the Kameyama-koen Park, or go into the Tenryu-ji Temple and to the Bamboo Grove.
  • The Arashiyama bamboo grove is a beautiful trail of tall bamboo stalks,
  • you will feel like you're walking in a different world.
  • There are 2 main hotels up in Arashiyama called the Hoshinoya Kyoto ($$$-$$$$) more on the traditional japanese side,
  • and also the Suiran ($$$$) more modern but with tradition Japanse features.







  • Fushimi Inari Taisha Shrine

  • This is a temple where many people go to pray and draw fortunes.
  • There are two paths with thousands of red vermilion torii gates
  • in a row leading into a trail. It's a beautiful place to
  • take a nice relaxing walk.
