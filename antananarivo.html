Antananarivo, Madagascar


About Antananarivo
(18.8792° S, 47.5079° E)
Time zone: Madagascar standard time zone GMT (GMT+3:00)
Currency: Malagasy ariary (1 Malagasy ariary = 0.00027 USD)Antananarivo is the capital city of Madagascar.
Its main features are tombs of royalties and wooden houses, there are many mosques and royal palaces.
Visitors may not be able to visit all of the royal palaces and landmarks.
Although there are lists of landmarks below for tourists
Climate
Summer in Antananarivo starts in December and ends in March,
the weather is pretty humid in summer (50°F - 80°F),
there may be some rainfall around March towards the end of the summer.

Spring season in Nairobi starts in September and ends in November,
(51°F - 80°F) it is still quite warm but it can also be a little chilly at times.
The humidity might still be existent.

Fall season starts in March and ends in May (53°F - 78°F).
These months rain the most especially in April, there can be 8 inches of rain!
April is known as the short month of rain, however march and may are known as the long months where it is constantly raining.

Winter season starts in June and ends in September (49°F - 67°F) .
The winters are not too cold but there can be some days that are chilly. The weather is pretty dry.



Landmarks





  • Botanical and Zoological Garden of Tsimbazaza

  • This is a zoo and peaceful botanical garden,
  • located in the southern Antananarivo.
  • Many rare animals and plants are living in the zoo and gardens.
  • It is a very peaceful atmosphere.
  • There is also a museum that offers visitors to look
  • at the collection of natural history exhibits.





  • The Rova of Antananarivo (queens palace)

  • This is a royal palace in Madagascar.
  • It served as a home to sovereigns in the kingdom of
  • Imerina in the 17th and 18th centuries,
  • it also served the rulers of the kindo in the 19th century.














  • Andafiavaratra Palace

  • the palace used to be a resident of the Prime Minister, Rainilaiarivony.
  • He governed the island kingdom in the late 19th century.
  • It is now a museum that mostly holds the broken parts
  • of Rova’s building that were rescued from a fire in 1995.

















  • Ambohimanga

  • This place is a traditional fortified settlement,
  • it is located a little far from the city, but it is a nice place to visit and experience.
    •
  • It is the most important spiritual and historic sites for the Malagasy people,
  • and it is built on one of the twelve sacred hills of Imerina.
