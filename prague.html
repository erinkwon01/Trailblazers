Prague, Czech Republic


About Prague

(50.0755° N, 14.4378° E)
Time Zone: Central European Summer Time (GMT+2)
Currency: Koruna


Climate

Warm summers (70°s-80°s)
Cool winters (40°s-50°s)


Culture

Family life takes precedence over social things.
Strong work ethic - hard working.
Social roles are defined - men make the money, women are expected to prioritize childcare over work (given 4 years maternity leave).
Czechs are polite and non confrontational - hesitancy may indicate politeness or an indirect way of saying no.
When dining out, round tip up to the nearest 50 or 100 CZK
Meetings: Firm handshakes, Saying “how are you” is seen as intrusive and impolite by the recipient.






Landmarks




  • Gamcheon Culture Village

  • Gamcheon Culture Village is a village filled with colorful houses all throughout the village.
  • You can also observe a lot of different types of artwork as you walk down the different laneways.
  • When you arrive at the top of the hill, you can see a beautiful view of the village and the colorful hues of the houses.
    •









  • Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

  • Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is among the few temples that are located on the coast rather than inland.
  • You can get a nice view of the beautiful ocean while basking in the Korean culture that is held within this temple.
  • In addition, there are many locals that also visit this temple, causing this to be a more immersive experience.







  • Haeundae Beach

  • Haeundae Beach is the most popular beach in Busan.
  • There is a wide variety of activities you could do on this beach, like sunbathing, swimming,
  • use a banana boat or a jet boat, kayak, paddleboard, and much more.
  • You can just as quickly walk right down to the city to get some food and still see the ocean.
  • This beach is very popular and can get crowded quite quickly.










  • Jalgachi Fish Market

  • Jalgachi Fish Market is a popular seafood market in South Korea, which is so successful due to the fact that Busan is
  • one of the biggest seaports in South Korea. Including both an indoor and outdoor section, there are lines and lines of fresh,
    •
  • living, dried, and cooked seafood. You may be shocked at just how loud and busy this market is, but this fish market is one to visit for a trip to Busan.
